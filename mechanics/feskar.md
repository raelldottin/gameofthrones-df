## Active Command — Calculated Assault

Each round:

- 20% chance to reduce the non-Basic-Attack Physical Damage Dealt of the enemy with the highest Strength by **12%** for 2 rounds.

On rounds 2, 4, 7, and 9:

- deal Tactical Damage to the enemy with the fewest troops;
- displayed Damage Rate: **+100%**;
- Tactical Damage scales with Feskar's Instinct and is mitigated by the target's Intelligence.

At 6★ or higher, through Emerald Inferno, on rounds 3, 5, 8, and 10:

- deal Fire Damage to all enemies that deal non-Basic-Attack Physical Damage;
- base Damage Rate: **+40%**;
- against Burn targets, the damage is multiplied by 1.5, producing a **+60%** displayed Damage Rate;
- Fire Damage scales with Feskar's Intelligence and is mitigated by the target's Initiative.

## 2★ — Resilient Bond

Locked in the observed 1★ state; Feskar needs one more star.

At the start of combat:

- grant Feskar and one adjacent ally one stack of Resilient Bond.

At the start of each round:

- if that ally retreated during the previous round, grant Feskar one additional stack.

Each stack reduces Physical Damage Received, excluding Basic Attacks, until the end of combat.

## Development Breakpoints

- **Current 1★:** Calculated Assault already provides repeated Tactical damage and a chance to suppress the strongest Physical-command enemy.
- **2★:** Resilient Bond adds persistent protection for Feskar and one adjacent partner.
- **4★:** Insightful Allies supplies an army-wide Instinct increase.
- **6★:** Emerald Inferno adds multi-target Fire punishment against Physical-command enemies.
- **8★:** Quick-Witted improves Feskar's Fire scaling and Initiative.
- **10★:** Unyielding Grasp adds long-duration control that prioritizes Warriors.

## Current Investment Decision

Feskar is only **11 relics** from 2★, making Resilient Bond the immediate progression target. The screenshot shows 10 Epic cores, 80 Champion Habit cores, and 725 Gold.

Do not spend Gold merely to force a Habit upgrade. Complete the 2★ relic requirement through guaranteed relic sources, then evaluate Resilient Bond after its actual upgrade cost is visible.
