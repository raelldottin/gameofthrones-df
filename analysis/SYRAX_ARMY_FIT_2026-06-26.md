# Syrax Army Fit — June 26, 2026

Source: Dragon Pit screenshots supplied June 26, 2026.

## Confirmed Current State

Syrax is a level 39, 1★ Legendary Sentinel Hatchling with 25,400 displayed power.

- Command: **Blazing Fury**
- Relic progress: 0/40
- Speed: Very Fast
- Positive troop affinities: Spearmen and Archers
- Negative troop affinity: Siege

## Current Command Role

**Blazing Fury** combines early support with scheduled Tactical damage:

- Each round, 20% chance to increase one ally's Fire Damage Dealt by 10% and grant First-Strike for 2 rounds, prioritizing a Fire-damage ally.
- Rounds 1, 4, 6, and 9: +110% Tactical Damage Rate against one adjacent enemy.

Syrax is already useful at 1★ because its command does not depend on an unlocked Habit. Its Very Fast speed and 172.8 Initiative support an early-tempo formation.

## Current Five-Army Placement

Keep the current formation:

- **Left:** Tashix
- **Vanguard:** Syrax
- **Right:** Antares
- **Troops:** Archers

No immediate five-army composition change is required. Syrax has an Archer affinity, high displayed power, early Tactical damage, and a Fire-support proc that can benefit Tashix or Antares.

## Star Breakpoints

### 2★ — Mindful Synergy

At the start of combat, increases Intelligence and Instinct for all three allies, enhanced by Syrax's Initiative.

This is Syrax's first major breakpoint because the formation contains Fire and Tactical damage. Intelligence improves Fire output, while Instinct improves Tactical output. At Habit level 1, both stats receive a displayed 6.5% increase before the Initiative enhancement is considered.

**Priority:** High. Syrax needs 40 relics and currently has 0.

### 4★ — Flight Mastery

Raises Initiative for all three allies and lowers Initiative for all three enemies for the entire battle. The effect is enhanced by Instinct.

This creates a full-team tempo swing and fits Syrax's Very Fast profile.

### 6★ — Strategic Revival

Adds Recovery on rounds 2, 5, and 8 for the ally with the least troops:

- Base Recovery Rate: 50% at Habit level 1
- Recovery rises to 75% when any enemy is Slowed
- 40% chance to grant Resistance for 2 rounds

The Recovery remains valuable even without Slow support. The enhanced 75% value requires a dependable Slow source in the formation or matchup.

### 8★ — Tactical Inferno

For the first three rounds, buffs Tactical Damage Dealt for an ally prioritizing the left flank and Fire Damage Dealt for an ally prioritizing the right flank.

At this breakpoint, review Army 4's flank assignments. The best use is a Tactical-damage dragon on the left and a Fire-damage dragon on the right. Antares already fits the right-flank Fire role. The left slot may eventually need reevaluation if Tashix cannot exploit the Tactical buff efficiently.

### 10★ — Mother's Mercy

Each round, can remove two Negative effects and one Control effect from an ally, prioritizing an ally under Control. The listed Control effects are Stun, Stagger, Overwhelm, and Confusion.

This turns Syrax into a stronger anti-control support, but it is a distant investment target.

## Investment Recommendation

The practical target is **2★ Mindful Synergy**. It offers immediate, team-wide value and reinforces both damage types used by the formation. The later 4★ and 6★ breakpoints are also strong, but they require much more relic investment.

The screenshots show 10 Legendary rarity cores, 142 Sentinel Habit cores, and 675 Gold. Syrax's current obstacle is obtaining 40 relics for 2★ rather than Habit materials. Do not chase the event chest from 0/40 unless the expected Gold cost becomes favorable or a guaranteed relic source appears.
