## Performance diagnosis

1. **Troop count was the biggest practical issue.** The player side entered with 16,571 troops against 21,146. Fill closer to the 21,205 cap before using this army into Lv. 8 resource defenders.
2. **Troop-type disadvantage made the result worse.** Shieldbearers dealt -7% damage while Cavalry received +7% damage. That swing matters over 10 rounds.
3. **Vhagar needs more protection in this matchup.** Vhagar died, reducing later-round pressure and control.
4. **Damage output was close enough to be encouraging.** The player side still removed 9,034 enemy troops despite the bad setup.
5. **Do not break the core based on this one report.** Malachite / Vhagar / Rhysarion remains a strong Shieldbearer core, but it should not be pushed into Cavalry defenders while underfilled.

## Immediate recommendations

1. **Do not use Shieldbearers into Cavalry for Lv. 8 Wood unless the army is full.** Choose a different tile, wait for a non-Cavalry defender, or use a Spearmen army if one is strong enough.
2. **Fill the army before retesting.** This formation should be tested near 21,205 / 21,205 troops.
3. **Keep Malachite / Vhagar / Rhysarion together.** The stalemate came under bad conditions, not from a clear formation flaw.
4. **For resource-defender farming, prioritize survivability around the vanguard.** Vhagar dying first suggests future upgrades should protect Sentinels and Shieldbearer armies before chasing small damage increases.
5. **Use this battle as a benchmark.** A future retest with full troops and neutral or favorable troop type should beat this result.
