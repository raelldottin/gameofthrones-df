## Habit Strategy

### 1. Do not spend Gold on this upgrade yet

Forest's Instinct is important, but the limiting material is not Gold. The limiting material is **Legendary Cores**. Spending Gold here does not complete the upgrade because the account is still short 190 Legendary Cores.

### 2. Keep Forest's Instinct as a high-priority upgrade once materials exist

Forest's Instinct is worth upgrading because it belongs to the reference gold-standard army:

**Rhysarion / Vhagar / Malachite — Shieldbearers**

It supports the full engine by improving the two other allies, which usually means Vhagar and Rhysarion. That makes it stronger than a selfish single-dragon habit.

### 3. Do not buy Sentinel Breedmarks with Gold until Legendary Cores are ready

The account is only 18 Sentinel Breedmarks short, but 190 Legendary Cores short. Buying Breedmarks now creates no immediate upgrade. Wait until the Legendary Core gap is closed, then decide whether covering the last few Breedmarks with Gold is worth it.

### 4. Legendary Cores are now a bottleneck resource

Future market priority should treat **Legendary Core x5 for 100 Campaign Favors** as one of the best default buys when available. This does not solve the full 190-core gap immediately, but it moves the account toward actual habit completion instead of wasting Gold on partial material coverage.

### 5. Upgrade order among current gold-standard habits

2. **Shimmer — Crushing Force** if affordable with available materials, because the Cavalry army needs damage support.

The upgrade is strategically good. The problem is timing and cost.
