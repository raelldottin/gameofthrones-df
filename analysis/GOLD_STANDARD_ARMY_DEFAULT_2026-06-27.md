# Gold-Standard Default Army — June 27, 2026

## Default Formation

**Rhysarion left / Vhagar vanguard / Malachite right**

**Troops:** Shieldbearers  
**Affinity goal:** 3/3

This formation is the default reference engine for judging every other army. It is not based on placing a Sentinel in every army. It is based on how complete the battle engine is.

## Why This Is the Default

A top army needs more than class balance. It needs:

1. a vanguard that can survive center pressure;
2. an early-round stabilizer;
3. team amplification or enemy suppression;
4. enough damage to finish enemies;
5. full troop affinity unless battle reports prove a broken-affinity setup is stronger.

This army has all five.

## Role Breakdown

### Vhagar — Vanguard

Vhagar remains the center piece because it can absorb direct pressure while contributing with Fiery Bonds. Do not move Vhagar out of vanguard unless repeated battle reports show that it cannot survive center pressure.

### Rhysarion — Left Flank

Rhysarion is the stabilizer. Ebbing Fury suppresses early damage and buys the army time to survive the opening rounds. Rhysarion's later Recovery timing is part of why the core holds together instead of collapsing early.

### Malachite — Right Flank

Malachite is the support and damage amplifier. Forest's Instinct can improve the other two allies' Physical damage while reducing Tactical damage received. In the Dock test, Malachite also produced meaningful captured damage through Warden's Rally.

## Evidence From Recent Battle Reports

The Lv. 2 Dock report showed the core trading slightly ahead against a larger Spearmen army. At the end of the captured Round 2 window, the player side went from 19,977 to 18,833 troops while the enemy went from 22,587 to 21,412. That means the player lost 1,144 troops while the enemy lost 1,175, a positive trade of 31 troops despite the player entering the round down 2,610 troops.

The same report showed the major parts of the engine working:

- Rhysarion's Ebbing Fury suppressed early damage.
- Malachite's Forest's Instinct triggered and gave Vhagar/Rhysarion +8% Physical damage and -8% Tactical damage received.
- Vhagar stayed in center and kept contributing through Fiery Bonds.

## Default Rule For Future Armies

Do not build by class quotas.

The goal is not one Sentinel per army. The goal is five strong engines. A candidate army should be compared to this standard:

| Requirement | Gold-standard answer |
|---|---|
| Center tank | Vhagar |
| Early stabilizer | Rhysarion |
| Support/amplifier | Malachite |
| Damage output | All three contribute, with Vhagar and Malachite adding command pressure |
| Sustain/timing | Rhysarion Recovery timing |
| Affinity | Shieldbearers 3/3 goal |

## Practical Rules

1. **Keep Vhagar in vanguard** unless battle reports prove otherwise.
2. **Use Rhysarion / Vhagar / Malachite as the default orientation** when enemy lane pressure threatens Rhysarion on the right.
3. **Allow flank swaps only for matchup reasons.** Older versions used Malachite left / Vhagar / Rhysarion right; that remains a valid test shape, but this file records the updated default requested on June 27.
4. **Do not break 3/3 troop affinity for theorycraft.** Broken affinity must prove itself in battle.
5. **Judge every other army by whether it has the same complete-engine structure**, not by whether it contains a Sentinel.
