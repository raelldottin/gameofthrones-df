## Mechanical notes

1. **Rhysarion's Ebbing Fury is doing real early-round work.** The -27.5% damage suppression was applied broadly in Round 1 and kept both sides' burst lower.
2. **Malachite triggered Forest's Instinct immediately.** That gave Vhagar and Rhysarion both offensive and Tactical defensive value.
3. **Enemy Syrax is a major support signal.** Mindful Synergy was visible on Syrax, Venator, and Crimson. That means enemy Syrax should be treated as more than a damage dealer.
4. **Enemy Venator pressured Rhysarion even while attacking Vhagar.** Venator's Basic Attack hit Vhagar, then Feral Strike reached Rhysarion twice.
5. **The core traded well despite the troop deficit.** Losing 1,144 while removing 1,175 is a good Round 1 sign when starting down 2,610 troops.

## Suggestions

1. **Keep Malachite / Vhagar / Rhysarion together.** This partial report supports the current locked-core recommendation.
2. **Keep Vhagar in vanguard for this core.** Vhagar absorbed the direct Venator Basic Attack and had self damage-reduction active.
3. **Expect Rhysarion to draw pressure from Venator and Syrax.** Rhysarion was targeted by Syrax, Venator's Feral Strike, and later Dawnsong exchanges. Its survival matters because Ebbing Fury is the early-round stabilizer.
4. **Target or disrupt enemy Syrax when possible.** Mindful Synergy boosted the whole enemy trio, so Syrax is a priority support piece even when Venator looks more dangerous.
5. **Capture the final screen next time.** The Round 1 trade is positive, but the final outcome is needed to decide whether this army can reliably clear Lv. 2 Dock defenders at this troop count.
