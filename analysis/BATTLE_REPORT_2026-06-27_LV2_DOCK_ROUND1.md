## Mechanical notes

1. **The core is trading ahead, but only slightly.** A +31 troop trade in Round 2 is positive, but it is not enough by itself to guarantee a clean win unless later-round sustain or enemy collapse improves the curve.
2. **Rhysarion remains the pressure point.** Syrax keeps hitting Rhysarion from the right lane, and Venator's Feral Strike can also reach Rhysarion.
3. **Malachite is doing meaningful damage.** Warden's Rally was the largest captured player hit in Round 2 at 295 Tactical damage.
4. **Vhagar is still the correct vanguard for this version.** Vhagar is soaking Venator's Basic Attack and still contributing damage through Fiery Bonds.
5. **Crimson is not harmless.** Bloodscale Terror hit Vhagar for 318 Fire damage, which was the largest captured enemy hit in Round 2.

## Suggestions

1. **Keep the current core as the default:** Malachite left / Vhagar vanguard / Rhysarion right remains valid.
2. **For this specific Dock defender, test a flank swap:** Rhysarion left / Vhagar vanguard / Malachite right. The reason is lane pressure: enemy Syrax is right flank and is repeatedly hitting Rhysarion. Putting Malachite into that lane may protect Rhysarion while preserving Vhagar center.
3. **Do not move Vhagar out of vanguard yet.** Venator is center, and Vhagar is the best current center answer in this core.
4. **Prioritize survival before extra burst.** The trade is already slightly positive. More incoming-damage reduction or Rhysarion survival may convert this from narrow/stalemate into a win.
5. **Capture Round 3 and Round 4 next.** Rhysarion's early suppression can make the first three rounds look slow. The later rounds decide whether the army actually finishes the defender or stalls.
