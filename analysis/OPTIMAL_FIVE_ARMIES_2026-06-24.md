## Army 1 — locked optimal Shieldbearer core

**Malachite left / Vhagar vanguard / Rhysarion right**

This is the strongest documented defensive formation in the current roster.

- Vhagar holds the center with Fiery Bonds control and Physical command damage.
- Malachite's Forest's Instinct has a 35% chance each round to increase the Physical command damage and reduce the Tactical damage received of the other two allies.
- Rhysarion's Ebbing Fury suppresses damage during rounds 1–3 and applies team Recovery at the start of round 4.
- All three receive Shieldbearer affinity.

Do not split this formation to strengthen another army.

## Army 2 — Velar-centered sustain

**Arrax left / Velar vanguard / Nyrena right**

Velar is the correct vanguard because its kit is built around team survival and center-lane control:

- Recovery to all three allies on rounds 2, 4, 6, and 8.
- Taunt access through Lure.
- Damage reduction and Strength through Dragon's Valor.
- Tactical damage and an ally damage increase through Whirlwind.

Arrax remains on a flank because Headlong Into Danger trades defense for offense. Nyrena stays protected on the opposite flank and supplies Tactical damage and debuffs.

## Army 3 — Seasmoke-centered Cavalry control

**Arulix left / Seasmoke vanguard / Shimmer right**

Seasmoke is the highest-level Legendary in this formation and is the safest vanguard choice.

- Arulix reduces enemy Fire damage, attacks enemies using Physical commands, and can Stagger targets in any lane.
- Shimmer reduces enemy Strength and Initiative.
- All three receive Cavalry affinity.

Arulix and Shimmer may switch flanks for a specific opponent. Place Shimmer opposite the enemy damage dealer whose Strength and Initiative most need to be reduced.

## Army 4 — Fast Archer burst

**Tashix left / Syrax vanguard / Antares right**

Syrax remains vanguard because Tashix and Antares are the two protected damage dealers.

- Tashix provides repeated Fire attacks.
- Antares deals Fire damage and applies Vulnerable, increasing target damage received.
- The formation is the only current army displayed as **Fast**.
- All three receive Archer affinity.

The current formation is underfilled by **6,017 Archers**. Fill the army to 18,275 before using battle results to compare it with the other formations.

## Army 5 — Crimson Spearmen control

**Jagadrix left / Dawnseeker vanguard / Crimson right**

Dawnseeker is the Sentinel and belongs in the center. Crimson remains protected because it is level 30, 1-star, and Slow, while Bloodscale Terror can target any lane.

- Jagadrix lowers enemy Instinct and Initiative.
- Crimson's Fire damage is mitigated by enemy Initiative, so Jagadrix's Initiative reduction improves Crimson's effective damage.
- Crimson adds odd-round Stun chances and Fire burst on rounds 2, 5, and 8.
- All three receive Spearmen affinity.

## Deployment priorities

1. Check troop matchup before every attack; do not rely on affinity alone.
2. Fill Army 4 before serious testing.
3. Keep Velar, Seasmoke, Syrax, and Dawnseeker in their vanguard positions.
4. Preserve Malachite, Vhagar, and Rhysarion as a locked core.
5. Flip flank dragons only when targeting a specific opposing lane.

## Suggested offensive use order

1. **Army 4 — Archer burst:** fastest opener when fully staffed.
2. **Army 1 — Shieldbearer anchor:** strongest overall sustain and control.
3. **Army 3 — Cavalry control:** use against Shieldbearers and Fire/Physical-command teams.
4. **Army 2 — Shieldbearer sustain:** attrition and recovery.
5. **Army 5 — Spearmen Crimson control:** anti-Cavalry and developing Fire burst.

Use this order as a functional guideline, not as a reason to accept troop disadvantage. A favorable troop counter should take priority over the nominal army order.
