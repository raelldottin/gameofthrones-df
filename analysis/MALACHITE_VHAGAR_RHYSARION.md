# Malachite / Vhagar / Rhysarion Shieldbearer Core

Source: player battle reports and Dragon Pit screenshots supplied June 23–24, 2026.

## Locked Formation

| Position | Dragon | Primary function |
|---|---|---|
| Left flank | Malachite | Team protection and Physical command support |
| Vanguard | Vhagar | Front-line control and Physical damage |
| Right flank | Rhysarion | Early damage suppression and round-4 Recovery |
| Troops | Shieldbearers | Positive affinity for all three dragons |

This formation should be treated as a fixed sustain/pressure core in the five-army roster.

## Shared Affinity

Malachite, Vhagar, and Rhysarion all have positive Shieldbearer affinity. When deployed with Shieldbearers, each dragon receives the observed **+20% to Dragon Stats** affinity bonus.

## Skill Interaction

### Rhysarion — Ebbing Fury

At habit level 1:

- Start of round 1: reduce Damage Dealt by all enemies and allies by **-27.5%** for 3 rounds.
- Start of round 4: apply **+25% Recovery** to all 3 allies, enhanced by Rhysarion's Strength.

This suppresses early burst from both sides and creates a reliable recovery checkpoint at the start of round 4.

### Malachite — Forest's Instinct

At habit level 1, each round Malachite has a 35% chance to buff the 2 other allies for 2 rounds:

- **+8% Physical Damage Dealt**, excluding Basic Attacks.
- **-8% Tactical Damage Received**.

In this three-dragon formation, the recipients are always Vhagar and Rhysarion.

### Vhagar — Fiery Bonds

Vhagar occupies the vanguard and supplies front-line control plus Physical command damage. Forest's Instinct directly increases Fiery Bonds' Physical damage when active, partially offsetting Ebbing Fury's temporary allied damage suppression.

## Combat Sequence

### Rounds 1–3: Suppress and survive

- Ebbing Fury reduces enemy burst damage.
- Vhagar controls the center lane and absorbs pressure.
- Forest's Instinct can reduce Tactical damage received by both flanks and improve Vhagar's Physical command damage.
- Full Shieldbearer affinity increases the baseline stats of all three dragons.

### Start of round 4: Recover

- Rhysarion applies Recovery to all three allies.
- Ebbing Fury's three-round damage-dealt suppression has ended.
- The army enters the remaining rounds with restored health and normal outgoing damage.

### Later rounds: Pressure

- Vhagar continues Physical pressure and control.
- Forest's Instinct can continue buffing Vhagar's command damage and protecting both allies from Tactical attacks.
- Rhysarion remains protected on the right flank and contributes Dawnsong damage.

## Why the Symmetrical Damage Penalty Favors This Team

Ebbing Fury affects allies as well as enemies, but this formation is designed to benefit from a slower opening:

- Vhagar and Malachite are durable enough to exploit the reduced early damage.
- Rhysarion is protected until the round-4 Recovery trigger.
- Malachite adds mitigation and partially restores Vhagar's Physical command output.
- The opponent's early burst window is reduced before the team heals.

## Upgrade Guidance

### Ebbing Fury

Upgrade cautiously. Higher levels increase Recovery but also suppress the team's own early damage more heavily. This is attractive for defensive PvP but may increase stalemate risk in ten-round offensive battles.

### Forest's Instinct

This is a safer sustain-core upgrade because each level improves both Vhagar's Physical command damage and the Tactical protection of Vhagar and Rhysarion without reducing allied output.

The observed level-2 requirements were:

- Ebbing Fury: 100 Epic cores, 100 green cores, and 70 Gold.
- Forest's Instinct: 100 Legendary cores and 100 green cores; no Gold cost was visible.

## Current Assessment

This is the strongest documented defensive Shieldbearer formation in the current roster. Keep Malachite left, Vhagar vanguard, and Rhysarion right rather than splitting them across other armies.
