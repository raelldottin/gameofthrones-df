# Arrax Army Fit — June 26, 2026

Source: Dragon Pit screenshots supplied June 26, 2026.

## Confirmed Current State

Arrax is a level 40, 2★ Rare Warrior Whelp with 15,450 displayed power. The active command is **Sudden Strike**, and **Headlong Into Danger** is unlocked at Habit level 1.

Confirmed positive troop affinities:

- Shieldbearers
- Archers

## Current Role

Arrax is a delayed Physical-damage attacker rather than a safe vanguard.

**Sudden Strike**:

- On rounds 2, 4, 6, and 8, has a 25% chance to apply Weakened to one adjacent enemy for 2 rounds. The chance rises to 50% against a Bleeding target.
- On rounds 2, 4, 5, 6, and 8, deals Physical Damage to two adjacent enemies at a +40% Damage Rate.

**Headlong Into Danger** activates at the start of round 4 for four rounds:

- Strength +25%
- Initiative +10%
- Physical Damage Dealt +25%
- Physical Damage Received +10% at the current Habit level
- Instinct -40% at the current Habit level

The round-4 burst is strong, but the simultaneous defense penalties make Arrax vulnerable during the same four-round window. Keep Arrax on a flank and pair it with a durable support vanguard.

## Best Current Formation

- **Left:** Arrax
- **Vanguard:** Velar
- **Right:** Nyrena
- **Troops:** Shieldbearers

Velar remains the preferred partner because its Recovery, Taunt, damage reduction, and Strength support compensate for Arrax's self-inflicted vulnerability. Nyrena supplies Tactical damage and debuffs from the opposite protected flank.

No immediate five-army composition change is required from this Arrax update.

## Star Breakpoints

### 4★ — Stone Bulwark

At the start of combat, reduces Tactical and Fire Damage Received for the other two allies until the end of combat. This strengthens Arrax as a flank protector without requiring it to occupy the vanguard.

### 6★ — Adaptive Guard

At the start of round 4 for four rounds:

- When leading **Archers**, reduces Tactical Damage Received for all three allies.
- When leading **Shieldbearers**, reduces Fire Damage Received for all three allies.

This timing exactly overlaps Headlong Into Danger's four-round burst window. It does not remove Arrax's Physical-damage vulnerability, but it improves formation survival against Fire or Tactical teams while Arrax is committed to offense.

### 8★ — Fire Ward

Grants Arrax and one adjacent ally a permanent Fire Ward stack. Each stack reduces Fire Damage Received. Additional stacks can accumulate on Arrax after the linked ally retreats.

### 10★ — Turn the Line

At the start of round 4, increases Physical Damage Received by two adjacent enemies for four rounds. This aligns with Arrax's own round-4 Physical burst and makes Arrax substantially more valuable in a Physical-focused formation.

## Investment Notes

Headlong Into Danger becomes safer as its Habit level rises. The offensive bonuses stay fixed, while the drawbacks improve from +10% Physical Damage Received and -40% Instinct at level 1 to no penalty at level 5.

The observed upgrade cost for Habit level 2 is 100 blue Rare cores and 100 green Warrior Habit cores. The screenshots show 24 Rare cores and 182 matching Habit cores, so the limiting material is Rare cores. Do not spend Gold to bypass that shortage.

## Formation Flexibility

Arrax can also operate with Archers because Adaptive Guard supports that troop type, but the current Archer army has stronger role-specific candidates. Keep Arrax with Velar and Shieldbearers unless a matchup specifically demands the Archer version.
