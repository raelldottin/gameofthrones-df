## What This Means

### 1. This orientation survived, but did not finish

**Nyrena / Velar / Arrax** kept the player ahead on raw troops by the end, but it did not remove the enemy army by round 10. For a true top-performing gold-standard army, survival alone is not enough. The army also needs finishing pressure.

### 2. Velar remains the correct vanguard

The center choice is not the first problem. Velar remains the safe vanguard for the Shieldbearer sustain army. The issue is whether Nyrena-left / Arrax-right is the best damage alignment.

### 3. The enemy spike rounds are the main warning

The player side got hit hardest on rounds **3**, **6**, and **9**:

- Round 3: player lost **3,406**, enemy lost **1,279**.
- Round 6: player lost **2,119**, enemy lost **857**.
- Round 9: player lost **1,924**, enemy lost **886**.

Those round numbers line up with Caraxes-style burst timing. The enemy Caraxes was in vanguard, so the Shieldbearer army needs better mitigation or faster pressure before those burst windows.

### 4. The army had good mid-round trades, but not enough

The player traded ahead in rounds **2**, **5**, **7**, **8**, and **10**. The best round was Round 5, where the player lost **902** and removed **1,607** enemy troops. That means the engine can trade well, but it loses too much value during burst rounds.

## Recommendation

Do not mark this orientation as a finished PvP gold-standard answer yet:

**Nyrena left / Velar vanguard / Arrax right**

It is acceptable as a survivable test shape, but not top-performing. The next clean test should swap the flanks while keeping Velar in vanguard:

**Arrax left / Velar vanguard / Nyrena right**

Reason:

1. Velar should stay center because it is the safe vanguard.
2. Arrax is the main Physical pressure piece and may need the left-side pressure role.
3. Nyrena may be better on the right as the debuff/control finisher.
4. The current orientation survived but failed to finish despite starting with a major troop advantage.

## Strategic Takeaway

For the five-army goal, this report keeps **Arrax / Velar / Nyrena** in the gold-standard candidate pool, but downgrades **Nyrena / Velar / Arrax** as the PvP default.

The army needs one of three improvements before it can be called top-performing in PvP:

1. better flank alignment;
2. more anti-burst preparation/survival;
3. stronger finishing upgrades for Arrax and Nyrena.

For now, keep **Velar vanguard**, but retest with **Arrax left / Velar / Nyrena right**.
