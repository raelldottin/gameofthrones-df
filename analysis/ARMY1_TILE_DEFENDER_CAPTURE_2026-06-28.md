# Army 1 Tile Defender Capture — Shieldbearers vs Spearmen

Date: 2026-06-28  
Source: 6 player-provided battle report screenshots  
Target: Tile Defender Lv. 6 Food, Pinkmaiden (1010, 1332)  
Attacking army: Rhysarion / Vhagar / Malachite with Shieldbearers  
Defender: Grim Warrior / Brazen Warrior / Cunning Hunter with Spearmen  
Result sequence: Stalemate, then Victory and territory capture

## Executive read

This set confirms that Army 1 can finish a tile after the main attrition fight leaves the defender nearly dead. The first visible tile-defender report was still a stalemate because of the 10-round cap, but the defender had only 107 / 6,000 troops left. The follow-up report was a clean Victory and claimed the Lv. 6 Food territory.

The key point is that this happened with Vhagar already dead. Rhysarion and Malachite were enough to preserve the fight state, clear the last troops, and supply siege damage.

## Dragon performance

### Rhysarion

Rhysarion remained productive after the main battle had already depleted the army.

- Stalemate report: 2,248 total kills
- Dawnsong: 6 casts, 869 kills
- Basic attack: 10 attacks, 1,379 kills
- Victory cleanup: 3,068 siege damage

Decision: Rhysarion is not a spare dragon. He is one of the reasons the shell still functions after Vhagar drops.

### Vhagar

Vhagar was already at 0 HP in both visible tile-defender reports.

- Stalemate report: 0 kills, 0 Fiery Bonds casts, 0 basic attacks
- Victory cleanup: 0 kills, 0 attacks, 0 siege damage

Decision: Vhagar remains part of the high-ceiling Army 1 shell, but these reports show that the late-fight cleanup does not depend on him once Rhysarion and Malachite have already created the advantage.

### Malachite

Malachite was the closer.

- Stalemate report: 3,645 total kills
- Warden's Rally: 6 casts, 1,128 kills, 286 healing
- Basic attack: 10 attacks, 2,517 kills
- Victory cleanup: 1 attack, 107 kills
- Siege damage: 6,932

Decision: Malachite should stay locked. This is not filler support; it is late-fight cleanup, sustain, and siege value in one slot.

## Defender read

The defender was weak compared with the army's main PvP target, but the report is still useful because it shows how the army behaves after a prior attrition fight.

Defender army:

- Grim Warrior Lv. 23 — Eclipse Fervor, no dragon stat bonus
- Brazen Warrior Lv. 23 — Lure, +20% dragon stats
- Cunning Hunter Lv. 23 — Cunning Whispers, +20% dragon stats

The defender did not threaten the remaining Rhysarion / Malachite pair enough to stop the claim.

## Strategic conclusions

1. Army 1 is now validated not only as an attrition shell, but also as a territory-capture shell.
2. The 10-round cap can cause a misleading stalemate when the defender is effectively finished.
3. Malachite is the clearest late-fight closer in this report.
4. Rhysarion is a locked Army 1 piece because he keeps producing after Vhagar is down.
5. Vhagar's value is front-loaded. The army needs him for ceiling, but the cleanup data says the shell can still finish without his late-fight participation.
6. Vaeldra should not replace Rhysarion or Malachite. The lower-army Vaeldra theory is stronger after this report.

## Practical rule added

When Army 1 produces a stalemate with the defender below roughly 500 remaining troops, a follow-up hit can be worth it. The first report here left only 107 defender troops, and the next hit immediately claimed the Lv. 6 Food territory.
